# 🌍 Introduction

This project focuses on the design, construction and experimental testing of a reusable **0.5-liter water rocket**.

Water rockets provide an excellent demonstration of fundamental engineering and physics concepts, including pressure, thrust generation, aerodynamics and projectile motion.

Besides building the rocket itself, the project aims to document the complete engineering process—from concept and CAD design to manufacturing, testing and evaluation.

---

# 🎯 Project Objective

The objective is to design and manufacture a stable and efficient water rocket capable of achieving significant altitude while maintaining aerodynamic stability throughout its flight.

The project also aims to investigate how different construction parameters influence overall flight performance and to apply theoretical physics in a practical engineering context.

---

# ❓ Research Question

> **How do rocket design, water volume and internal air pressure influence the flight stability and maximum altitude of a water rocket?**

---

# 📚 Theoretical Background

## ⚙️ How a Water Rocket Works

A water rocket stores energy by compressing air inside a partially water-filled plastic bottle.

When the launch mechanism releases the pressure, the compressed air rapidly forces the water through the nozzle.

The expelled water generates thrust that accelerates the rocket upwards. After all water has left the bottle, the rocket continues climbing due to its momentum until gravity eventually slows it down.

---

## 🚀 Newton's Third Law

The propulsion system is based on **Newton's Third Law of Motion**:

> **For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.**

As water is expelled downward at high velocity, an equal force pushes the rocket upward, creating thrust.

---

# 📐 Rocket Design

The rocket is based on a standard **0.5 L PET bottle** acting as the pressure vessel.

Additional aerodynamic components improve stability and flight efficiency.

### Planned Components

- 🔺 3D-printed nose cone
- 🪽 Four aerodynamic fins
- 🧴 0.5 L PET bottle
- 🔩 Nozzle
- 🚀 Launch coupling
- 🎯 Optional parachute recovery system

# 🛡️ Safety

The following safety precautions are observed throughout the project:

- Launch only in open outdoor areas
- Maintain a safe distance from spectators
- Inspect all components before every launch
- Never exceed the bottle's maximum pressure rating
- Never stand directly above the rocket during pressurization
- Always wear safety glasses
- Never aim the rocket at people or animals
- Perform launches only under adult or teacher supervision
